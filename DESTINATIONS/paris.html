How to Get to Paris from the Philippines

Traveling from the Philippines to Paris involves booking a flight from major international airports such as Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) in Manila. Airlines like Philippine Airlines, Air France, and Qatar Airways offer flights to Paris, typically with layovers in the Middle East or Asia. The average flight duration is approximately 16 to 20 hours, including layovers. It's advisable to check visa requirements, travel advisories, and health protocols before planning your trip.

Other Places to Visit in Paris

Paris is a city rich in history and culture, offering numerous attractions beyond the Eiffel Tower. Here are some notable sites to consider:

  • Louvre Museum - Home to thousands of works of art, including the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo.
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral - A masterpiece of French Gothic architecture with stunning stained glass windows.
  • Champs-Élysées and Arc de Triomphe - A historic avenue leading to a monumental arch honoring those who fought for France.
  • Montmartre - A historic and artistic neighborhood offering panoramic views of the city from the Sacré-Cœur Basilica.
  • Seine River Cruise - Experience Paris from the water, passing by landmarks like Notre-Dame, the Louvre, and the Eiffel Tower.

Eiffel Tower

Gyeongbokgung Castle
The Eiffel Tower (*La Tour Eiffel* in French) is a wrought-iron lattice tower located on the Champ de Mars in Paris. Constructed between 1887 and 1889 as the centerpiece for the Exposition Universelle (World's Fair) celebrating the centennial of the French Revolution, it was initially met with criticism but has since become a global cultural icon and a symbol of France. Standing at approximately 1,083 feet (330 meters), it was the tallest man-made structure in the world until 1930. Today, it attracts millions of visitors annually who come to admire its architectural brilliance and panoramic views of Paris.
